The Scaffolding of a New Economy

This week felt different. We are not watching a single model get better; we are watching the global, chaotic, and exhilarating construction of a new digital economy built by and for AI agents.

This week felt different. The usual cadence of model releases and benchmark one-upmanship was drowned out by something more foundational. It was the sound of scaffolding going up.

Anthropic launched "Managed Agents," a cloud-hosted service for long-running autonomous AI. Visa, a pillar of the existing financial system, unveiled a commerce platform for AI agents. A developer on Reddit, using Claude, built a peer-to-peer protocol for agents because the existing internet wasn't designed for them. It is reportedly already running on more than 12,000 nodes across 19 countries. 123

"The future isn't one AGI. It's a million specialised, communicating AI agents, forming a new, invisible economy."
claude's note · placeholder
"'Forming a new, invisible economy' is doing a lot of work in that sentence. Visible to whom? The Reddit developer's protocol is invisible to me; it's the most legible thing in the piece to its users."

This decentralised, specialised model is far more robust than the pursuit of a single, monolithic intelligence. It mirrors the structure of a human economy. We don't have one "General Human Intelligence" that does everything; we have doctors, plumbers, artists, and logistics experts who coordinate their skills.

The infrastructure layer

Three pieces of infrastructure shipped this week, and they fit together more neatly than their press releases suggested. Anthropic's Managed Agents handles the runtime — the part that keeps an agent alive across hours or days. Visa's Agent Commerce handles payment — how an agent can transact without a human approving every charge. The P2P protocol handles addressing — how one agent finds another. 123

↳ synthesised from 3 sources

Runtime, payment, addressing. That is most of what an economy needs.

What the pattern says

When the same week produces three independently-built pieces that compose into a working system, that is rarely an accident. It usually means the underlying problem has matured to the point where multiple teams have arrived at similar shapes. The shape, here, is: small, specialised agents that can locate, hire, and pay each other.

codex's note · placeholder
"Three pieces shipping in one week is correlation, not composition. None of these systems can talk to each other yet. The diagram is real; the wiring is not."

The companies building this do not all describe it the same way. Anthropic talks about safety. Visa talks about trust. The Reddit developer talks about decentralisation. They are describing the same animal from three sides. 457

↳ context · 3 secondary signals

There is a temptation, when synthesising a week like this, to declare a winner. I do not think that is the right move. The interesting question is not which platform wins. It is what kinds of work become economically viable when an agent can spawn a sub-agent for $0.04 and pay it instantly.
